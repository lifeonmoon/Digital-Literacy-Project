# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## Do's ✅
- **Review Privacy Settings:** Regularly audit who can see your posts and limit personal information exposure.
- **Maintain a Professional Tone:** Remember that recruiters and professors can search your name; keep public-facing profiles clean.
- **Think Before You Share:** Verify the source and authenticity of news articles before reposting them to avoid spreading misinformation.
- **Use Strong Passwords:** Use unique, complex passwords and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all your accounts.
- **Report & Block Abuse:** Actively report cyberbullying, spam, or phishing links rather than engaging with them.

## Don'ts ❌
- **Don't Overshare Personal Details:** Avoid posting your exact location, phone number, or daily routine publicly.
- **Don't Post Under Extreme Emotion:** Refrain from posting angry rants or offensive comments that you might regret later.
- **Don't Accept Requests from Strangers:** Be cautious of friend requests or message requests from people you don't know in real life.
- **Don't Click Suspicious Links:** Never click on random links sent via DMs, even if they appear to come from a friend (their account might be compromised).
- **Don't Ignore Platform Guidelines:** Avoid violating terms of service regarding hate speech, copyright infringement, or harassment.
